# Abstract

The Operational Worlds Conceptual Model (OWCM) provides a systems-level view of the Operational Worlds framework.

Where the Operational Worlds Engineering Proposals (OWEPs) define individual concepts, the OWCM illustrates how those concepts relate to one another throughout the research lifecycle.

The model serves as the conceptual map linking theory, methodology, engineering, and research.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of the Operational Worlds Conceptual Model is to describe the relationships between the core concepts of the Operational Worlds framework.

It answers the question:


**How do the concepts of Operational Worlds interact to produce research?**

---

# 2. Layers of the Framework

The framework consists of five interconnected layers.

## Research Layer

Defines why an Operational World exists.

Research

↓

Research Question

↓

Hypothesis (optional)

---

## Design Layer

Transforms a research question into an Operational World.

Operational World

├── Operational Families

├── Primitives

├── Relationships

├── Rules

└── Constraints

---

## Interaction Layer

Participants engage with the Operational World.

Primitive

↓

Capability

↓

Participant Action

↓

Mechanism

↓

Operational State

Capabilities define what is possible.

Participant Actions realize those possibilities.

Mechanisms describe the dynamic processes that emerge from participant actions over time.

Operational States represent the condition of the world at any given moment.

---

## Observation Layer

Operational activity produces evidence.

Operational State

↓

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Results

Observations may include participant behavior, operational metrics, media production, or other measurable outcomes.

---

## Reflection Layer

Research continues through reflection.

Results

↓

Reflection

↓

New Research Questions

↓

New Operational Worlds

The framework is recursive.

Each Operational World contributes to future research.

# 3. Supporting Systems

The following concepts support Operational Worlds but are not currently considered part of the core ontology.

Examples include:


• User Interface.
• Infrastructure.
• Database.
• Storage.
• Authentication.
• Networking.
• Media Formats.

These may vary across implementations without changing the conceptual structure of an Operational World.

---

# 4. Candidate Concepts

The following concepts remain under investigation and may become part of the ontology after sufficient validation.

Current candidates include:


• Resource.
• Participant Strategy.
• Session.
• Persistence.
• Interface Object.

Candidate concepts should only become formal ontology after repeated validation across multiple Operational Worlds.

# 7. Future Development

The Operational Worlds Conceptual Model will evolve alongside the framework.
